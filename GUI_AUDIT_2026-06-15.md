# GUI Audit — AorticCenterlineApp

**Date:** 2026-06-15
**Scope:** Full audit of the EVAR Planner GUI (`+app/AorticCenterlineApp.m`, ~10.7k lines) and the packages it drives (`+autoseg`, `+preprocess`, `+evar_plan`, `+ifu`, `+io`, `+vmtk_centerline`, `+ui_helpers`, `run_planner_headless.m`). Covers: all 6 steps end-to-end (User-driven + Automatic), code quality & bug scan, clinical correctness of EVAR outputs, UI/robustness, and code formatting/documentation.
**Method:** three static-analysis passes (bugs/error-handling, Step 5–6 wiring, formatting/docs) + live functional testing of the app on the JohnDoe1 and JohnDoe2 CTs + clinical sanity-check of the derived measurements.

---

## Executive summary

The **core pipeline is sound** — segmentation, branch detection, CFA-capped distal endpoints, and the bifurcated VMTK centerline now run reliably end-to-end on both real scans (108/108 regression tests green). The defects are concentrated in **(a) the GUI's downstream steps (Analyze/Export) and cross-step state handling, and (b) clinical measurement semantics**, plus the expected maintainability debt of a 10k-line single-class app.

Findings are grouped by severity. **Critical/High items that were safe to fix have been fixed and verified this session** (Section A). The clinically-loaded items that require *your* decision before changing outputs are in Section B. Maintainability items are in Section C.

Severity counts: **Critical 3 (all fixed), High 9 (5 fixed, 4 need decision/scoping), Medium ~14, Low ~15.**

## B. Needs your decision (clinical correctness — not changed yet)

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-15.** B1, B2, and B3 were adjudicated by the operator and
> implemented: the two engines are unified through `evar_plan.measure_from_centerline`
> (on-screen == export); neck angulation now reports both α (suprarenal-to-neck) and
> β (infrarenal neck-to-sac, the IFU-canonical angle); all diameters are tagged/labeled
> lumen-Ø (excludes mural thrombus); neck length is N/A + `aneurysm_detected=false`
> when no aneurysm onset fires. Non-GUI regression 108/108 green. See CHANGELOG
> 2026-06-15 and GOALS.md. B4–B6 below remain open.

These affect the numbers an operator reads/exports, so I did **not** unilaterally change them — they need a vascular-surgeon call on the intended definition.

### B1 — [HIGH] Two different measurement engines give different numbers for the same case
The **on-screen Step 5 measurements** use `preprocess.evar_measurements`, while the **exported plan** (Step 6 / `runIFUMatch`) uses `evar_plan.measure_from_centerline`. On JohnDoe1 they disagree materially:

| Quantity | Step 5 display | Exported plan |
|---|---|---|
| Neck angulation | **62.6°** | **9.3°** |
| Neck length | 82 mm | 121–131 mm |
| Max diameter | 24.7 mm | 29–34 mm |

An operator sees one angulation on screen and exports another — and 62° vs 9° flips device eligibility. **Recommendation:** pick one engine as authoritative and have both the display and the plan call it. I can reconcile once you tell me which definition you want (or I can diff the two and propose the correct one).

### B2 — [HIGH] AAA diameter is lumen-based and underestimates the true sac
Max "aneurysm" diameters came out 30–34 mm on both cases — these are **contrast-lumen** diameters (TS segments lumen only). In a thrombus-laden AAA the outer-wall sac can be far larger. As-is, the tool can under-call aneurysm size and neck/seal zones. **Recommendation:** outer-wall/ILT segmentation (the AortaSeg24 direction already in GOALS) or, interim, an explicit "lumen diameter — excludes mural thrombus" label on every diameter so it isn't mistaken for sac diameter.

### B3 — [HIGH] "Neck length" is implausible when no discrete aneurysm is detected
Neck lengths of 84–131 mm appear because the aneurysm-onset detector (radius threshold) doesn't fire on these (small-lumen) cases, so the "neck" runs to the bifurcation. **Recommendation:** when no aneurysm onset is found, report neck length as N/A (or flag "no aneurysm detected") rather than a long number that reads as a real neck.

### B4 — [MEDIUM] Bifurcation angle looks off / engine-dependent
Reported aortic bifurcation angle was 115° (JohnDoe1) and 60° (JohnDoe2); the pinned JohnDoe2 baseline elsewhere is 28°. The tangent-sampling is sensitive and changed when the iliacs were CFA-capped. Worth validating against a manual measurement on a known case before relying on it for angulated-iliac device screening.

### B5 — [MEDIUM] Neck angulation is infrarenal-only
`measure_from_centerline` measures suprarenal-vs-infrarenal tangents but not the **suprarenal fixation zone** angulation that suprarenal-fixation devices (e.g. Zenith) care about. No device currently encodes a suprarenal-angulation limit, so no present impact — a blind spot to note.

### B6 — [LOW] IFU library is research-grade
7 devices; thresholds are from peer-reviewed summaries (Chaikof 2018, AbuRahma 2018), **not vendor IFUs** (correctly disclaimed in the plan). The bifurcation-angle criterion is a placeholder (all devices NaN, so never enforced). Fine for research; must be reconciled with vendor IFUs before any clinical use.

## C. Other findings (not blocking; recommended)

### C1 — Error handling / robustness (Medium–High)
- **Silent CPR fallback** (`~line 4847`): on failure CPR renders a dummy −1000 HU image, indistinguishable from a valid black pane. Should show an explicit "CPR generation failed" state. *(Secondary view; left as-is, documented.)*
- **~25 `try/catch` blocks that only `fprintf`/swallow** across viewer3d setup, overlays, status updates. Individually minor, collectively they hide failures from the operator. Recommend surfacing user-facing ones via the status bar/`uialert`.
- **Modal blocks for automation**: `uiconfirm` in the Step-2 audit and the first-launch tour block a scripted/headless run. The one-click `runAutoPipeline` already avoids them; only relevant if you script the per-step buttons.
- **Weak seed-bounds validation** (`~line 8490`): `app.D.vol(SeedSeg…)` indexed without a validity check in one path (other paths clamp).

### C2 — Step 5/6 wiring (Medium)
- Step 5 IFU verdict is **modal-only / not persisted**; Step 6 re-derives it. Consider storing it on the app so Export shows the same verdict the operator just saw.
- **Mesh export advertised but not implemented** (Step 6 placeholder text lists `.stl`; no button). Either wire `evar_plan.export_mesh` or drop the claim.
- **Auto-export doesn't add to library** (User-driven does). Minor parity gap.
- Export coordinate **round-trip drift**: GUI exports re-derive mm from voxel polylines (`centerline_to_mm`), giving slightly different neck Ø (e.g. 19.9 vs 21.8 mm) than the cached headless `out.plan`. Small, but a source of "why don't these match" confusion.

### C3 — Maintainability / formatting / docs (Medium–Low)
- **Monolith**: one ~10.7k-line class, ~184 methods, little sectioning. Longest methods that should be decomposed: `refreshVolViewer` (~366 lines), `renderMeasurementsOn` (~260), `runSegmentation` (~230), `liveGrowFromSeed` (~150), `drawCenterlineOverlay` (~120). Recommend `%% ====` section banners and extracting helpers.
- **Duplication**: the `switch app.ViewMode` projection block is repeated 15+ times (~300 lines); `voxel→mL` and axis/`daspect` setup repeated inline. Extract `projectToView`, `voxelVolumeToML`, `clearTag(ax,tag)`.
- **Magic numbers**: HU thresholds (75, 25, 600), spatial windows (50 mm supraceliac, 30 mm CFA cap, 8-vox bbox), layout (410-px side panel), seed colors repeated inline. Promote to a `Constant` properties block.
- **Undocumented public API**: `injectCT/injectMask/injectSeeds/injectCenterlines` and several 80–360-line private methods lack headers (the `+preprocess` package's docstring style is a good template to copy).
- **Naming**: mostly consistent camelCase, but a few `snake_case` methods (`render_gated_step_placeholder`, `step_mode_toggle_render`); view-mode strings (`'3d'`,`'3dvol'`,`'cpr'`,`'2x2'`) are unguarded literals. Inconsistent `arguments`-block vs `nargin` and error-ID schemes across files.
